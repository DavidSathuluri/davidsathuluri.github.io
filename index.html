If AI Knows the Planet Is Dying, Why Can’t It Tell Us Who’s Drowning?
Common Dreams

In this article, I dealt with the limitations of AI in planetary health monitoring, emphasizing the need to embed environmental justice and equity into AI systems to ensure they protect vulnerable communities and do not perpetuate existing power imbalances. It highlights issues such as proprietary data, lack of local community involvement, and the need for integrating social vulnerability indicators alongside environmental data. The article calls for co-designed, just, and inclusive AI frameworks for effective and equitable climate action

Reading Columbia’s Colonial Campus: How Architecture Shapes Student Consciousness
The MorningSide Post, SIPA|Columbia

The artilce is exploring on how Columbia University’s campus architecture embodies and perpetuates colonial and imperial narratives that shape student consciousness, reinforcing Western European intellectual authority while marginalizing Indigenous and non-Western knowledge. The article examines the symbolic power of architectural design in maintaining social hierarchies, and highlights student protests that challenge and rewrite these spatial narratives as acts of decolonization and resistance. It calls for developing "landscape literacy" to critically engage with and transform how educational spaces communicate power and knowledge.

About

I'm a researcher and writer based in New York City, currently pursuing a Master of Arts in Climate and Society at Columbia University's Climate School. My work focuses on the intersections of climate justice, caste inequality, human rights, and decolonial thought.

As a Research Assistant at Columbia University's Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory and Climate School, I investigate how climate vulnerabilities are shaped by existing social hierarchies, particularly examining the climate-caste nexus in South Asian contexts. I also serve on the Review Committee at Columbia's Institute for the Study of Human Rights.

My writing and research appear in publications including Down To Earth, The Ambedkarian Chronicle, Columbia Climate School, The Quint, Breakthrough News, Maktoob Media, Convergence Magazine, and Common Dreams. I write about climate policy, environmental justice, artificial intelligence ethics, and the political economy of climate finance from Global South perspectives.

Previously, I served as Project Consultant for the Global Forum of Communities Discriminated on Work & Descent (GFoD), researching caste-based discrimination and climate vulnerability. I hold a degree from the University of Delhi and am a Fellow working at the intersection of inclusive climate action, AI, and human rights.

My approach bridges rigorous academic research with activist-oriented journalism, employing decolonial theoretical frameworks to challenge dominant narratives in climate discourse and amplify marginalized voices in environmental governance.
