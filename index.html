About

I'm a data journalist and reporter based in New York City with a Master's degree in Data Journalism from Columbia University. I focus on stories that use data to illuminate human rights issues, climate change impacts, and political developments.

My work has appeared in international publications including The Guardian, New Lines Magazine, The New Humanitarian, and The Diplomat. I specialize in combining traditional reporting methods with data analysis to uncover stories from marginalized communities.

In 2024, I was honored to be selected as a finalist for the Thomson Foundation's Young Journalist Award. My investigative piece on enforced disappearances in Balochistan for Kontinentalist received the SOPA Award for excellence in data-driven journalism.

I'm passionate about using both traditional reporting methods and data analysis to uncover stories that might otherwise remain untold, particularly from marginalized communities. My technical skills include Python, R, data visualization, mapping with Mapbox, and responsive web design for storytelling projects.

About

I'm a climate justice researcher and policy analyst based in New York City, currently working as a Research Assistant at Columbia University's Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory and Climate School. I hold an MA in Climate and Society from Columbia University.

My research focuses on the intersections of climate change, caste inequality, and environmental justice in the Global South, with particular emphasis on South Asia. I investigate how climate vulnerabilities are shaped by existing social hierarchies, and how climate adaptation and finance mechanisms often reproduce colonial patterns of exploitation.

As a freelance writer and investigative journalist, my work has appeared in publications including The Guardian, The Intercept, Common Dreams, Jacobin, The Nation, and Middle East Eye. I write about climate policy, environmental justice, state violence against activists, and the political economy of climate finance.

I'm passionate about bridging academic research with activist-oriented journalism to amplify marginalized voices and challenge dominant narratives in climate discourse. My work employs rigorous fact-checking, data analysis, and decolonial theoretical frameworks to expose power dynamics in environmental governance.
